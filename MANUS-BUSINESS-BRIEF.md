## Open questions — where we want Manus to push back

These are unresolved tensions where another set of eyes would be valuable:

### 1. The refund guarantee — keep / drop / reframe?

Peter said this morning: *"lets not guarantee that we already going to show how we can improve and free up space etc?"*

- **Keep**: it's the single line competitors can't match. Strongest differentiator on a cold sales call. Concrete, falsifiable.
- **Drop**: replace with consultative framing — *"we'll show you how we can improve operations and free up time"*. Softer, less risky for us.
- **Reframe**: keep falsifiable promise but tie to operational outcome — *"If Metis Cortex doesn't free up 5+ hours of phone work in your first month, full refund"* OR *"We'll show 3 operational improvements in 14 days, or refund."*

**What's your read?** A leveraged boutique with 1 founder selling to peers — does the guarantee help conversion or telegraph desperation? Is the consultative framing strong enough alone, or is it just every competitor's pitch?

### 2. Three-tier stack — right architecture or premature?

We've spec'd Tier 1 (Receptionist), Tier 2 (Knowledge), Tier 3 (Outbound). Tier 3 was added 2026-05-08 after a Belle Property Manly customer-discovery call where the agent said *"finding buyers"* (not "answering questions") was the #1 real estate pain.

Concern: **0 paying external clients** and we already have 3 SKUs. Discipline rule says don't build Tier 2/3 until 1 paying Tier 1 client. But the architecture exists in docs.

**What's your read?** Should we collapse messaging to "Metis Cortex" only (single product, Receptionist only) until we have 5 paying clients on Tier 1? Or does showcasing the upsell path help close the first 5 (because high-LTV ICPs see the room to grow)?

### 3. ICP prioritization — who's Case Study #3?

DSK = cleaning (Case Study #1). Eonia = aesthetic clinic (#2). Both are own businesses. **Case Study #3 must be external** — first paying client.

Candidates:
- **Boutique RE agency** (e.g. Belle Property Manly, McGrath, Atlas) — high ARPU potential (Tier 2 + 3), Peter has warm contacts
- **Dental practice** (1–10 chairs, owner-led) — best fit for ICP filters, missed-call pain is acute, but no warm contacts yet
- **Cleaner peer** — fastest to close (Peter knows the cleaning market intimately), but lowest ARPU and risk of "you only do cleaning"
- **Beauty clinic** — Eonia adjacency, strong vertical but Eonia case study covers it

**What's your read?** Which vertical does the leveraged boutique strategy convert fastest, and which has the longest LTV?

### 4. Pricing — too cheap, too expensive, or right?

A$1,500 setup + A$600/mo. ICP: AU SMBs 1–10 employees, owner-led. We landed here because:
- Sophiie / Chime are A$200–500/mo (lower spec, no done-for-you)
- Bespoke agency builds A$5k–15k setup + A$1k–3k/mo
- Our target operator can sign A$2,100 first cheque (setup + month 1) without finance approval

**What's your read?** Is A$600/mo too cheap for "done-for-you AI receptionist that books your missed calls" (suggesting a price floor of A$1,200/mo)? Or too expensive for cold cleaners / dental practices where A$300/mo is the mental anchor?

### 5. Naming — "Metis Cortex" vs "Metis"?

We just renamed (2026-05-09): "Frontline" was the product name; we dropped it because three names was overhead. Now: company AND product = **Metis Cortex**. Casual nickname in marketing copy: **Metis**.

Alternative we considered: **Metis** as the product name, **Metis Cortex** as the formal company name (parallels Stripe / Stripe Inc.).

**What's your read?** Is "Metis Cortex" too academic / heavy for SMB sales conversations? Should we lead with "Metis"?

### 6. Greek mythology positioning — when to deploy?

Currently: **off the homepage.** Peter's discipline says story (Metis = goddess of cunning + good counsel) lives on `/about` Phase 2 once 5 paying clients exist. Until then, no founder photos, no "Metis was the goddess of…" pull-quotes.

**What's your read?** Right call to delay, or are we losing a brand-feel lever that helps premium positioning?

### 7. Outbound spec — real product or shiny object?

[OUTBOUND-SPEC.md](OUTBOUND-SPEC.md) details Tier 3: AI calls existing customer database based on triggers (year-end recall, listing matches, abandoned inquiries). High-ARPU. Could be the real game.

But: **0 paying Tier 1 clients.** Discipline rule says don't build until 1 paying. Risk: spec rots, market moves, opportunity cost of focus.

**What's your read?** Is Outbound the actual product (and Tier 1 is the wedge), or are we kidding ourselves about a Phase 2 we won't ship?

### 8. Distribution / GTM realism

The wedge is *warm intro to peers* — Peter's network of cleaners, agents, dentists, beauty operators. No cold outbound, no paid ads, no content marketing. **Apollo enrichment is held until DSK pilot data lands**, then opens up cold email v1.

**What's your read?** Does warm-intro-only scale to 50+ clients, or does it cap around 10 and we need a second motion (paid, content, partner programs) by month 6? When should the second motion start, and which one?

### 9. Defensibility once at 5 paying clients

Sophiie / Chime can copy our pricing + refund guarantee in a quarter. They have engineering teams, AU brand spend, and existing distribution. What's the *real* moat once we've proven this works?

Candidates:
- **Own-business case studies** — DSK + Eonia (different verticals) is hard to fake
- **Speed of installation** — 14-day turnaround vs competitors' 30+ days
- **Vertical depth on Knowledge tier** — per-asset Q&A is harder to ship than receptionist
- **Operator-to-operator credibility** — trust signal but not a structural moat

**What's your read?** Force a pick on which is the *primary* moat and which are decorative. Would a competitor that copied our pricing + guarantee but had bigger marketing budget eat us alive?

### 10. Cash flow sensitivity

DSK + Eonia subsidise operations. Founder's living expenses come from the trust's other revenue. Metis Cortex itself: A$1,500 setup + A$600/mo per client.

Costs per client: ~A$200/mo Retell + Twilio + GHL infra; ~10–20 hr Peter time on install.

**What's your read?** How many paying clients before Metis Cortex is cashflow-positive standalone (not subsidised by the trust)? At what point does Peter need to stop running DSK ops himself and hire?

### 11. Refund guarantee — structural self-selection trap?

Current copy: *"5 extra appointments in month one or full refund."* But:
- Customer with 8 missed calls/month → 5 bookings is impossible → auto-refund
- Customer with 100 missed calls/month → 5 bookings is trivial → no refund risk

The guarantee as written **self-selects only high-volume operators.** Low-volume SMBs either don't sign (read the math) or sign and we owe them money.

**What's your read?** Should the guarantee scale to *their* baseline (e.g. *"we'll book 25% lift"* or *"we'll book everything we capture"*) rather than an absolute number? Or is the absolute number the right filter — only sell to high-volume operators?

### 12. GTM positioning conflict — receptionist vs ops layer

Two different framings live in the docs:

- **Design system + homepage:** *"AI receptionist. Picks up calls. Books appointments."* (concrete, narrow, easy to grasp)
- **Business brief + Outbound spec:** *"AI ops layer for SMBs"* (broader, upsell narrative, multi-tier vision)

**What's your read?** Which converts cold? Should the homepage say "AI receptionist" and sales conversations expand to "ops layer," or should one frame win across the whole funnel?

### 13. The hire-or-don't-hire moment

Peter is solo on Metis Cortex. Eonia therapist hire is for *Eonia* (free up Peter's clinical hours), not Metis Cortex.

At what client count does Peter need person #2 specifically on Metis Cortex? Roles candidates:
- **Installer** — handles 14-day setups so Peter can sell
- **Salesperson** — owns warm-intro pipeline + demos
- **Operations** — handles billing, support, monitoring

**What's your read?** Which role first, and what's the trigger client count?
