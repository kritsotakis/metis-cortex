## Read this first — the structural decision before terms

You said "hire on a profit split". That phrase covers three legally very different structures, and **the choice matters more than any single clause below**:

### Option A — Casual employee + discretionary profit-share bonus ✅ RECOMMENDED

- Therapist is a **casual employee** of Kritsotakis Investments Pty Ltd (as trustee for the trust)
- Paid **hourly at the Beauty Industry Award 2020** rate for their classification (typically Level 3 or Level 4 — confirm with lawyer based on qualifications)
- Plus a **monthly discretionary bonus** calculated as % of net profit attributable to their work
- You withhold PAYG, pay 11.5% super (rising to 12% from July 2026), provide casual loading (25% on top of base)
- Casual = no annual leave, no sick leave, no notice period beyond 1 hour
- After 12 months of regular pattern, casual conversion rules apply (Fair Work Act) — if they're working set hours weekly, they can convert to permanent

**Why this is the safest:** ATO and Fair Work Ombudsman cannot reclassify this. You're paying as employer, withholding correctly, and the bonus is a reward — not the basis of engagement. If the bonus arrangement falls apart, the employment continues at award rate with no legal mess.

### Option B — Independent contractor (ABN-based) ⚠️ RISKY

- Therapist invoices Eonia under their own ABN
- Paid a contractor rate (often higher hourly to cover their own super, leave, tax)
- "Profit share" = monthly performance bonus on top of fixed rate
- **Sham contracting risk:** Australian common law (Hollis v Vabu, Personnel Contracting v ZG) looks at the substance of the relationship, not the label. If the therapist works set hours at your premises, uses your equipment, follows your protocols, takes direction from you — they are an employee regardless of what the contract says. ATO will reclassify and you'll owe back-pay, super, and penalties.
- **Only viable if:** the therapist genuinely runs their own business, sets their own hours, brings their own clients, can refuse work, works for other clinics. None of that fits the Eonia setup.

**Recommendation: do NOT use Option B.** The savings on super and leave are eaten 10x by the back-pay risk.

### Option C — Partnership / joint venture 🚫 NOT FOR THIS HIRE

- Therapist becomes a co-owner of part of Eonia's business
- Contributes capital and shares losses (not just profit)
- Requires partnership agreement, separate ABN registration, partnership tax return
- Locks them in legally — exit becomes complex
- Massively expensive to unwind if it doesn't work

**Don't use this for a first therapist hire.** Partnerships are appropriate when both parties bring meaningful capital + IP + customer base. A therapist starting at your clinic doesn't.

### My recommendation

**Use Option A** — casual employee at award rate + monthly profit-share bonus. Everything below assumes Option A unless flagged otherwise.

### 6. Profit-share bonus (the upside)

This is the part where most agreements go wrong. Read carefully.

**Definition of "Profit" for bonus calculation:**

Net Treatment Revenue from services performed by the Employee, less:
- Cost of consumables for those services (itemised — products, single-use items, lasered consumables)
- Therapist's labour cost for those services (their hourly pay × time on the service)
- A 30% allocation toward clinic overhead (rent, utilities, insurance, equipment depreciation, software, admin)

**Bonus rate:**
- The Employee receives **___% of monthly Profit** as defined above
- Suggested starting rate: **20–30%** (negotiate based on experience, expected revenue contribution, and how long it takes to build a book)

**Worked example (illustrative, not committed):**
- Employee performs $20,000 of treatment revenue in the month
- Consumables: $2,000
- Their labour at award rate: $4,500 (assuming ~80hrs at $56/hr loaded)
- Overhead allocation (30% of revenue): $6,000
- Profit: $20,000 − $2,000 − $4,500 − $6,000 = **$7,500**
- At 25% bonus rate: **$1,875** monthly bonus on top of their $4,500 hourly pay
- Total month earnings: ~$6,375

**Bonus payment terms:**
- Calculated monthly within 14 days of month end
- Paid as a separate line item on the next pay run after calculation
- PAYG withheld on bonus (it's wages — not separate)
- Super applies to bonus per OTE rules

**Transparency:**
- Employer provides a one-page monthly P&L showing the calculation (revenue per service, consumables, hours, overhead allocation, profit, bonus)
- Disagreement: raise within 14 days of receiving the calculation; resolve via good-faith discussion before any external dispute

**Bonus discretion:**
- The bonus is **discretionary in payment** but not in calculation — i.e. the formula is fixed, but the Employer reserves the right to pause the bonus during pre-defined trigger events (e.g. clinic financial distress, clinical complaint under investigation, gross misconduct).
- Trigger events must be communicated in writing within 7 days of the decision.
- Pausing without a trigger event for >2 consecutive months entitles the Employee to terminate the engagement on 1 week's notice (rather than the casual 1-hour standard).

**This clause is the single most negotiated section of the agreement.** Get the lawyer's eyes on it.

### 9. Patient and staff non-solicitation (post-employment)

For **6 months** after employment ends, the Employee will not:
- Directly contact existing Eonia patients to encourage them to follow the Employee to a new business
- Recruit Eonia staff to leave for a new business

**Note on non-compete:** Australia's general courts are very reluctant to enforce non-competes that prevent someone working as a therapist at another clinic. Don't put a blanket non-compete in. Non-solicitation is the enforceable cousin and is what you actually need.

### 10. Termination

## What to do if the therapist pushes back

Common pushbacks and how to respond:

- **"I want a higher base rate."** Pay above-award if you want to. Cannot pay below. The bonus is what makes the package competitive — the base is just legal compliance.
- **"I want a guaranteed minimum bonus."** Decline. Guaranteed bonus = ordinary time earnings = changes super calc. Stay on hourly + discretionary bonus structure.
- **"Why a 30% overhead allocation?"** Because rent + utilities + insurance + software + your time managing the clinic is real cost. Be transparent — show them the actual P&L of the clinic for one month so they see the overhead is real.
- **"I want equity in Eonia."** Decline at this stage. Equity is for partners who contribute capital + IP. Therapists who deliver excellent treatments earn through profit share, not equity. Revisit at 12-month mark if the relationship is strong.
- **"Can I bring my existing patients?"** Welcome them — but those patients become Eonia patients (book through Eonia's system, pay Eonia's rates, fall under Eonia's records). The therapist takes profit share on their treatments, same as any other patient.
