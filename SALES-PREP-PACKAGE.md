## Standing principles (internalise once, apply every demo)

- **You're an operator, not a salesperson.** You ran **Limani Seafood Restaurant in Narrabeen for 21 years**. You run DSK Property Cleaning today. You run an aesthetic clinic (Eonia) and a chemistry lab (HydraLab). You built Metis Cortex because you needed it. The prospect is a peer. Talk that way.
- **Demo > deck.** They booked the call to hear Zoe answer a phone, not to watch slides. Don't open a slide deck. Ever.
- **Your strongest move is silence.** When they go quiet after the demo, *let them*. Quiet means they're calculating. Filling silence reads as needy.
- **Match their energy.** High-energy plumber — lean in, faster pace. Considered clinic owner — slower, more space between sentences. Don't push.
- **Say "I" not "we."** You're a solo founder. Owning the product personally is your differentiation against agencies that hide behind a "team."
- **The pitch is the demo + the stacked guarantees + the founding-rate scarcity, not your words.** When in doubt, dial the number again and let Zoe do the work.

## The Hormozi 4-step close (run this in every demo)

Every demo follows this structure. Don't skip steps. Don't reorder.

1. **Confirm the pain in numbers.** *"How many calls a week do you reckon you miss? × your average job/booking value = $X/month walking out the door."* They feel the math.
2. **Show the outcome live.** Dial the demo number. Zoe answers. Booking lands in calendar. They see Day-30-with-Metis in real time.
3. **Stack the value.** *"Here's what you get: 14-day install (worth A$5,000), AI receptionist 24/7 (worth A$4,000/mo), custom voice trained on your calls (worth A$2,000), CRM integration (worth A$1,500), monthly tuning (worth A$500/mo), transcription + routing (worth A$300/mo), Australian support (worth A$200/mo). Total stack: A$8,500 setup + A$5,000/mo. Your investment: A$1,500 setup + A$1,200/mo. Or A$800/mo if you take a founding-rate spot."*
4. **Risk-reverse with stacked guarantees.** *"If Zoe doesn't handle 100% of your missed calls and save you 10 hours of phone time in the first 30 days, full refund. AND if I don't go live in 14 days, the A$1,500 setup fee is waived. No paperwork. Both apply."*
5. **Reason-why close (founding rate).** *"There are 5 founding-rate slots — one per vertical. Cleaning's already going to DSK. Restaurant slot is open (I'd love to put it on Limani in Narrabeen as Case Study #3 — full disclosure, I ran the place for 21 years before selling). Real estate, dental, beauty also open. A$800/mo for life if you take a slot. After the 5th client, standard rate is A$1,200/mo with no founding lock. If your vertical's open, this is the moment."*

That's the close. Don't add. Don't soften. Let them respond.

## Section 1 — The 10-minute demo flow

### Minute 0:00–1:00 — Opening + frame

Open with their answers from the Calendly pre-call questions. Read them back literally — shows you read it.

Sample opening, replace bracketed parts with their actual answers:

> *"Hey [name], Peter from Metis Cortex. You wrote that you run a [their service business] and you reckon you miss about [their number] calls a week. Anything else worth me knowing before I show you what I built?"*

Listen for 30–45 seconds. Don't pitch yet. They might add context, or they might say "no, that's it." Either is fine.

Then set the frame:

> *"What I want to do is dial a real test number — Zoe, the AI receptionist, picks up. You'll hear her qualify a fake lead and book it. Takes about 3 minutes. Then we'll talk about whether it makes sense for your business or doesn't. Cool?"*

Wait for the yes. If they say something other than yes, address it before dialling.

### Minute 1:00–4:00 — Live demo (no slides, no deck)

Pull up your phone. Dial the Metis Cortex test number. Put it on speaker. Run a realistic enquiry — pick a vertical that **mirrors their business**, not always pre-sale cleaning.

If they're a tradie:
> *"Hi, I'm calling because I need a plumber out tomorrow morning, kitchen sink's blocked. North Bondi, three-bed unit, can you fit me in?"*

If they're a clinic:
> *"Hi, looking to book in for a hydrafacial. Have you got anything Wednesday late afternoon?"*

If they're an agent:
> *"I'm a buyer's agent looking at a property in Mosman, want to discuss off-market opportunities — can you give me a callback this afternoon?"*

Let Zoe do her thing. Don't narrate over her. Don't apologise for any moments where she pauses to process — those are 1-second pauses and the prospect won't notice unless you draw attention to them.

When the booking confirms, end the call. Show the prospect the SMS confirmation that just landed on your phone. Show them the entry that just appeared in your test calendar.

That's the entire demo. **Three minutes of product, one minute of dialling.** No slides.

### Minute 4:00–6:00 — Translate to their business

Don't ask "what did you think?" — that's an open-ended sales-pitch question and they'll feel obliged to be polite.

Instead, translate it directly:

> *"For your [their vertical], the script changes but the structure's identical. Your version of Zoe would ask [their qualifying question — e.g., 'how many bedrooms', 'which suburb', 'what kind of treatment', 'when do you need it']. You'd want her to capture [their specific data — pre-sale dates, treatment types, etc.]. The voice would still be Australian female, the tone still warm-and-efficient. Two-week build."*

Specificity matters here. If their pre-call answer mentioned a specific pain ("I miss calls during jobs at strata sites"), bring it up by name:

> *"Specifically for the strata problem you mentioned — Zoe handles those identically. You're on a roof, she picks up, she captures the building manager's details, books the visit."*

Don't ask if they're impressed. Move on.

### Minute 6:00–8:00 — Pricing + guarantee + 14-day install

Plain numbers. No fancy framing. No "investment" language.

> *"Pricing is $1,500 to install — that's the build, the integrations, the testing, the 14-day setup. Then $1,200 a month (or $800 founding rate, first 5 clients only) to run her. Month-to-month, no contract. Month one, if Zoe doesn't book handle 100% of your missed calls and save your team at least 10 hours in the first 30 days, full refund of the $1,500. No paperwork."*

Pause. Let them ask. If they don't ask, prompt:

> *"Anything that needs explaining there?"*

If they ask about the install timeline:

> *"Day 1 is the welcome email and three onboarding questions. By Day 7 you're listening to Zoe in test calls. Day 14 she goes live on your number. I run all of it — you do nothing apart from giving me the three onboarding answers and listening to a test call midway through."*

### Minute 8:00–9:00 — Their questions

Whatever they ask, the answer is short. Long answers leak desperation. See Section 2 for the 10 most likely.

If they ask something you don't know — *"how does this handle [edge case]?"* — say:

> *"Honestly, I'd need to check that for your exact setup. Want me to come back to you by tomorrow afternoon with a specific answer?"*

Better to defer than to invent. They'll respect honesty more than confident hand-waving.

### Minute 9:00–10:00 — Close

Three options. Always offer all three. Let them pick.

> *"Three ways to take it from here. One — book the install now, I send a Stripe link, we kick off Day 1 tomorrow. Two — send me one or two of your typical inbound enquiry recordings, I'll come back with a custom-tailored demo on a 15-minute follow-up so you hear Zoe doing your specific job. Three — sleep on it, talk to your business partner, jump back on a 30-minute call later this week. Which suits?"*

Wait for them to pick.

- **If they pick option 1 (book now):** *"Brilliant. I'll text you the Stripe link in 2 minutes. Onboarding email lands the moment payment processes."*
- **If they pick option 2 (custom demo):** *"Done. Send the audio to peter@kritsotakis.com.au or text it to 0423 668 766. I'll book a 15-minute slot for [day after tomorrow]."*
- **If they pick option 3 (sleep on it):** *"All good. I'll send a one-line follow-up Friday with the Calendly link. No chasing — if it's a yes you reply, if it's a no you ignore."*

End the call. Don't oversell. Don't add bonus information. Hang up first if you can without being rude.

## Section 2 — Objection handling (the 10 most likely)

For each: the **underlying concern** Peter should hear (not the literal words), the **response** (under 30 words spoken), and the **bridge** to keep the conversation moving.

### 1. *"It'll sound robotic / customers will hate AI"*

**Underlying concern:** brand reputation. They're worried their customers will think they've cheaped out.

**Response:**
> *"Want to hear her again? I'll dial the test number, you tell me when it sounds robotic. If it does, I owe you the call. If it doesn't, you owe me 30 seconds of honesty."*

**Bridge:** dial again. Let Zoe re-prove herself. After the second call: *"Modern voice AI crossed the line about 18 months ago. Most callers don't know they're talking to one until you tell them — that's the actual data."*

### 2. *"$1,200 a month (or $800 founding rate, first 5 clients only) is a lot"*

**Underlying concern:** ROI / cash flow. They want it to work, the price is the friction.

**Response:**
> *"It's roughly the cost of one missed booking a month for most service businesses. Zoe catches anywhere from five to twenty. Maths usually works in month one."*

**Bridge:** ask their average job value. Add their average phone-handling time per booking, multiply by 10 (the guarantee floor). Compare to $1,200. The maths makes itself. *"At [their job value × 10] vs $1,200/month, where does the maths land for you?"*

### 3. *"What if it screws up a real customer call?"*

**Underlying concern:** customer experience / liability. They're imagining the worst case.

**Response:**
> *"Every call recorded, weekly review with you, escalation to me by SMS for anything weird. If she fumbles a real one, we tune the prompt that day. Worst case is rare and recoverable."*

**Bridge:** *"That's also why month one is on the refund guarantee. If she's not delivering by week four, you're not paying for the install."*

### 4. *"Are you locking me into a contract?"*

**Underlying concern:** flexibility. They've been burned by SaaS lock-in.

**Response:**
> *"Month-to-month after the 14-day install. Cancel anytime, 30-day notice. The $1,500 install is the only one-off."*

**Bridge:** *"That's deliberate. If Metis Cortex doesn't earn its keep month-to-month, I lose you. Keeps me honest."*

### 5. *"My phone system is non-standard / VoIP / weird"*

**Underlying concern:** technical complexity. They're worried it'll be a nightmare to integrate.

**Response:**
> *"We bring our own number and forward your existing one to it. Works with anything — VoIP, mobile, landline, third-party PBX. I don't touch your phone system."*

**Bridge:** *"What's running it now?"* — listen for any panic in their answer. If they describe something wild, say *"happy to map it out for free, no commitment — what's a good window for a 15-minute scoping call?"*

### 6. *"I want to wait until you have more case studies"*

**Underlying concern:** risk aversion. They want social proof before committing.

**Response:**
> *"Fair. The first 5 clients get the founding-customer rate locked for 12 months — same $1,200/month, no annual lift. After that I'm putting the price up. So waiting costs you on price, not just on bookings missed."*

**Bridge:** *"And the refund guarantee handles the case-study gap. If month one doesn't deliver, you're whole. Worst case is you got a 14-day install for free."*

> Note: only deploy the founding-customer-rate framing if it's true. If you're not actually committing to a 12-month price hold for the first 5, swap this for: *"Refund guarantee covers the case-study gap. Worst case month one doesn't deliver — you're whole."*

### 7. *"My missed-call problem isn't that bad"*

**Underlying concern:** prioritisation / urgency. Metis Cortex isn't top of their pile right now.

**Response:**
> *"Cool. Worth doing one thing — count missed calls in your phone log this week. If it's under 3, you're right, skip it. If it's over 5, the maths flips."*

**Bridge:** if they hesitate to count: *"Want me to send a 60-second audit template? You text me the numbers Friday, I tell you yes or no. No pitch, just the data."*

### 8. *"What integrates with [their CRM]?"*

**Underlying concern:** tech fit. They've got a workflow they don't want to break.

**Response:**
> *"Jobber, ServiceM8, GoHighLevel, Cliniko, Pabau, Halaxy, Google Calendar, Outlook, anything with a webhook. What are you using?"*

**Bridge:** confirm their CRM is on the list. If not, say *"I'd need 24 hours to confirm, but webhooks usually crack it. Send me the CRM name and I'll come back tomorrow with a yes or no."*

### 9. *"How is this different from [Sophiie / Chime / cheaper competitor]?"*

**Underlying concern:** comparative shopping. They're price-checking.

**Response:**
> *"They sell software, you set it up. I sell the install, I set it up, I refund if it doesn't work. Different product."*

**Bridge:** *"If you've got the time and inclination to configure GoHighLevel and a voice AI yourself, save the $1,500. If you'd rather it just be done, that's where I sit."*

### 10. *"I need to think about it / talk to my partner"*

**Underlying concern:** deferral. They're not saying no, they're saying not now.

**Response:**
> *"All good. I'll send a one-liner Friday with the Calendly link. No chasing. If it's a yes you reply, if it's a no you ignore."*

**Bridge:** don't argue. Set up the soft follow-up. Then move on. *"Anything else I can answer before we wrap?"*

## Section 3 — Pricing rebuttals (5 specific scenarios)

Holding price discipline matters more than any single deal. Discounting before you have 5 paying clients sets a permanent floor that's hard to walk back from.

### 1. *"Can you do it for $X less?"*

**The rebuttal:**
> *"I get the ask, but I can't move on the install. The $1,500 covers real cost — Twilio number, GHL sub-account, Retell credits, 14 days of my time. Going below that means I'm losing money to win you, and that's not a relationship that ends well for either of us."*

**The concession that's actually a feature:**
> *"What I'll do is the refund guarantee — month one doesn't deliver, you get the $1,500 back. That's the discount, just paid out backward instead of forward."*

**The walk-away if they push:**
> *"If $1,500 is the blocker, Metis Cortex isn't the right fit right now. There's a self-serve tool called TransferToAI doing $99/month — less polished, you set it up, but it'd get you in the door. Happy to point you at them."*

> Pointing at a specific competitor (TransferToAI) when you walk away is counter-intuitive but it works. They remember you as the guy who told them the truth. Half come back in 6 months when self-serve hasn't worked. The other half were never your customer anyway.

### 2. *"What if I just buy the setup, skip the monthly?"*

**The rebuttal:**
> *"Won't work — there's no Zoe without the monthly. The $1,200 covers Retell credits, the GHL sub-account, the SMS volume, ongoing tuning, and Australian-hours support. Without it, the agent dies after week one."*

**The concession:**
> *"What I can do — pay 6 months upfront ($4,800), get month 7 free. That's the only bundling I do. Same install fee."*

> Only offer the 6-months-upfront discount if cash flow matters more than holding price discipline. If you're cash-positive, hold the price.

### 3. *"What about a 6-month commitment for a discount?"*

**The rebuttal:**
> *"Metis Cortex's already month-to-month, no contract. If I gave you a discount for committing 6 months, I'm trading flexibility you already have for money I don't need to take."*

**The reframe:**
> *"What I'd rather give you is the founding-customer-rate hold — same $1,200/month, no annual increases, locked for 12 months. That's actually worth more long-term than a 6-month discount."*

> Same caveat as Objection #6 — only deploy the founding-rate framing if it's a real commitment.

### 4. *"Pay me when it works, refund if not"*

**The rebuttal — they want pure performance pricing, you're already doing this:**
> *"That's literally what the refund guarantee is. You pay $1,500 upfront, but if month one doesn't book handle 100% of your missed calls + save 10 hours of phone time, the money comes straight back. Net cost zero if it doesn't work, $1,500 if it does. That's the same thing as performance pricing, just structured to keep me honest."*

**The bridge:**
> *"The reason it's structured this way and not pure pay-on-results — your billing system already knows how to handle a $1,500 invoice. Pay-on-results means I'm chasing you for performance fees forever. Cleaner this way for both of us."*

### 5. *"Can you give me 14 days free to test?"*

**The rebuttal — equivalent to skipping payment entirely:**
> *"The 14 days is the install — that's where the work happens. There's no Metis Cortex to test before then; we're building it for your business in those 14 days."*

**The reframe:**
> *"What you actually want is risk reversal. That's the refund guarantee — you pay the install, and if it doesn't handle 100% of your missed calls + save 10 hours, you're whole. Better than free trial because it puts skin in the game on both sides."*

**The walk-away:**
> *"If the only way you'd do this is free first, Metis Cortex's probably not the right fit yet. Wait until missed calls are costing you visibly more than $1,500. Then it's an obvious yes."*

## What you do NOT do on a sales call

- ❌ Open a deck. Ever.
- ❌ Walk through how the AI works under the hood. They don't care.
- ❌ Talk about the brand story / Greek etymology / "AI brain layer." Save for `/about` page.
- ❌ Promise specific outcomes ("Zoe WILL book you 12 extra jobs"). Promise the guarantee instead — that's truthful.
- ❌ Trash competitors by name. Acknowledge them when asked. *Never* attack them — you sound desperate.
- ❌ Discount before 5 paying clients exist.
- ❌ Add bonus features in the heat of a close ("I'll throw in...") — sets a precedent for every future call.
- ❌ Stay on the call past 10 minutes unless they're closing. Long demos cool deals.

## Post-call — within 30 minutes

Whatever the outcome, send a one-line text or email within 30 minutes:

- **They closed:** *"Brilliant — Stripe link incoming. Onboarding kicks off the moment payment lands."*
- **They wanted custom-tailored demo:** *"All good — drop the audio when you've got it. I'll book the 15-min for [day after tomorrow]."*
- **They wanted to think:** *"All good — Friday one-liner incoming. Whatever you decide is the right call for your business."*
- **They said no:** *"All good — appreciate the 10 minutes. If anything changes, you've got my number."*

The 30-minute follow-up is non-negotiable. Demo bookings have the highest cancel-from-the-other-side rate in the 24h after the call. Locking the next step kills that.
