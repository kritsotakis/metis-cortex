## The three candidates

### 1. LegalVision

- **Website:** https://legalvision.com.au
- **Employment law page:** https://legalvision.com.au/need-an-employment-lawyer/
- **Quote process:** Free quote form on site, or call their consult line (1300 544 755)
- **Email:** Use the website's "request a quote" form for fastest reply (their team triage routes by specialty)
- **Sydney office:** Yes, head office Sydney
- **Why this one:** Largest of the three by client volume; most likely to have a beauty/aesthetic clinic precedent in their casebook; modern intake process means you'll get a quote within 24 hours
- **Watch for:** Their fees can drift higher than Sprintlaw on complex matters — be explicit about the budget cap upfront

### 2. Sprintlaw

- **Website:** https://sprintlaw.com.au
- **Employment law page:** https://sprintlaw.com.au/employment-law/employment-lawyer/
- **Quick Review service:** https://sprintlaw.com.au/contracts/quick-review/
- **Quote process:** Free quote form on site, or book a call directly
- **Sydney office:** Yes, head office Sydney
- **Why this one:** Tightest small-business / startup focus of the three; explicit "Quick Review" fixed-fee packages are exactly the shape of engagement you want; usually the most price-competitive; their content marketing leans heavily into small-business employment which suggests this is a sweet-spot for their team
- **Watch for:** They're online-first like LegalVision — if you want a face-to-face, this isn't them

### 3. Coutts Legal

- **Website:** https://couttslegal.com.au
- **Employment law for employers:** https://couttslegal.com.au/employment-law/for-employers/
- **Sydney office:** Yes, plus offices in Wollongong and Campbelltown — proper NSW boutique
- **Quote process:** Phone first (you'll get a partner-level conversation faster than the online-first firms)
- **Why this one:** They explicitly position around employer-side employment law (correct side for your matter), which most Sydney employment lawyers don't — most are split between employee and employer practice; fixed-fee available; you'll get a more relationship-led service if that matters
- **Watch for:** Slightly less specifically positioned around tech/startup small business; more general SMB. Still a good fit for a clinic owner.

## How to evaluate the three responses

When the quotes come back, weight by:

1. **Specialisation confirmation** — did they answer "yes, we've done Beauty Award + profit-share before"? If yes, they're the front-runner regardless of price.
2. **Fee** — within the A$300–600 band. Anything over A$700 means they're not really fixed-fee for a 1-hour review.
3. **Turnaround** — under 5 business days for the review is reasonable. If anyone says "next week" without a specific day, push back.
4. **Communication style** — fast, plain-English replies are a strong signal. Long delays or jargon-heavy emails predict the engagement experience.
